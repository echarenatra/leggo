Glencoe & First Taste of the Highlands
Base Camp Hotel Nevis Range
Scenic drive
▾
Morning
  • 09:00 Check out from Stirling
  • Loch Lomond stop at Tarbet — stretch legs, take in the loch
  • Drive north on the spectacular A82
Afternoon
  • Buachaille Etive Mòr photo stop — the iconic pyramid mountain

  • ✂️ Glen Etive adds 45–60 min (14-mile dead-end road). Worth it for the scenery and deer, but this is the first thing to cut if you're running behind schedule.

Tue, 21 Apr 2026
Hogwarts & Monster Hunting
Base Camp Hotel Nevis Range
Kids highlight
▾

Thu, 23 Apr 2026
Skye — The Full Trotternish Loop
Hebridean - Rooms & Apartments
Full day
▾
Morning
  • 08:30 Early start from Sleat to beat the crowds
  • 09:30 Old Man of Storr — 30 min hike on a clear path to the base of the pinnacles. Manageable for a 6yo, no summit needed.
  • 11:30 Kilt Rock & Mealt Falls — quick roadside viewpoint (10 min)
Afternoon
  • 12:30 The Quiraing — walk the flat section of the path for mind-blowing views over the landslip landscape
  • 14:30 Fairy Glen near Uig — a bizarre miniature landscape of grassy spirals and rocky towers. Basically a natural playground for a 6yo.
Evening
  • Drive back south through Portree to Sleat (~1 hr)
  • Dinner at or near the hotel
Notes
  • ✂️ If weather is poor or energy is low: Kilt Rock is a quick 10-min roadside stop — the most skippable. Storr, Quiraing, and Fairy Glen are the three essentials of this day.
  • Fairy Glen: free, no booking. Small parking area near Uig — arrive before 16:00 to avoid the busiest period.
  • ℹ️ Dunvegan Castle has moved to Day 7 morning — it fits the West Skye day perfectly and removes the long backtrack from today.
  • ⚠️ April on the Trotternish ridge is fully exposed — waterproofs and warm layers are non-negotiable, especially for the 6yo.

Fri, 24 Apr 2026
Skye — West Coast: Castles, Pools & Black Sand
Hebridean - Rooms & Apartments
Kids highlight
▾
Morning
  • 08:30 Early start — drive from Sleat to Dunvegan (~50 min)
  • 09:30 Dunvegan Castle & Gardens — the oldest continuously inhabited castle in Scotland
  • Seal Colony Boat Trip — depart from the castle jetty, see wild grey seals up close
Afternoon
  • Drive south to the Fairy Pools (~30 min)
  • Fairy Pools — 40 min walk beside crystal-clear turquoise mountain pools. Perfectly manageable for a 6yo.
  • Pub lunch in Carbost
  • Talisker Bay — easy 20 min flat walk to a spectacular black sand beach enclosed by towering sea cliffs
Evening
  • Optional: Neist Point Lighthouse for golden hour (~20:45 sunset on 24 Apr). Drive ~25 min from Talisker, 40 min walk return to the lighthouse. Atlantic views, seabirds, dramatic skies. If the family has energy and the sky is clear — go.
  • Drive back to Hebridean. Prepare for a very early morning tomorrow.
Notes
  • 📋 Dunvegan Castle seal boat trips: runs from April but early season — call Dunvegan Castle ahead to confirm availability and operating days. Lower demand — no urgent pre-booking, but check closer to the trip.
  • ✂️ If time runs short: Talisker Bay is the most skippable — Fairy Pools already delivers the magical water experience. Dunvegan + Fairy Pools are the two essentials.
  • ⚠️ Day 8 requires a 06:30 departure. Pack tonight, set alarms, prepare coffee.

Titanic & Belfast History
ibis Belfast City Centre
Kids highlight
▾
Morning

Mon, 27 Apr 2026
The Causeway Coast
ibis Belfast City Centre
Full day
▾
Morning
  • 08:30 Drive the Causeway Coastal Route (A2) — one of the world's great coastal drives
  • 10:00 Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge — swinging bridge over a sea chasm. Pre-booked timed entry.
  • 12:00 Giant's Causeway — let the 6yo hop across the 40,000 interlocking basalt columns
Afternoon
  • 14:00 Dunluce Castle — dramatic clifftop ruins, quick photo stop
  • 15:00 Dark Hedges — Game of Thrones: The Kingsroad. Quick roadside stop — park carefully on the verge.
  • 15:45 Mussenden Temple + Downhill Beach — circular temple perched on a clifftop above the Atlantic. Beach below for the 6yo to run. On the route back to Belfast.
Evening
  • Drive back to Belfast (~1 hr)
  • ~18:30 Arrive Belfast — dinner near hotel
  • Pack for the move to Galway tomorrow
Notes
  • 🔴 MUST PRE-BOOK: Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge — timed entry, fills up fast even in April. Book via nationaltrust.org.uk/ni.
  • 📋 Giant's Causeway car park: pre-book via National Trust to guarantee access. The stones are free to walk — pre-booking is for the car park only.
  • Dark Hedges: free, no booking. Arrive off-peak for fewer people in photos.
  • ✂️ If running short on time: Dunluce Castle is a quick photo from the car — no entry needed. Mussenden Temple is the most optional stop but it's on the route back and takes only 30–45 min.

Tue, 28 Apr 2026
Winterfell, Newgrange & Galway
Maldron Hotel Sandy Road Galway
Epic day
▾
Morning
  • 08:30 Check out ibis Belfast
  • 09:30 Castle Ward (Winterfell) — put on Stark cloaks and do the Game of Thrones archery lesson in the courtyard. One of the most unique family experiences on this trip.
  • 11:30 Drive south, cross into the Republic of Ireland
Afternoon
  • 13:30 Newgrange (Brú na Bóinne) — pre-booked timed entry. Step inside a 5,200-year-old passage tomb older than Stonehenge and the Egyptian Pyramids. The tour includes a shuttle bus to the monument + guided walk (allow 1.5–2 hrs).
  • 15:30 Drive west to Galway (~2h45)
Evening
  • 18:00–18:30 Check into Maldron Hotel Sandy Road Galway
  • Head to Shop Street — busker watching, colourful streets
  • Pub dinner at Tig Cóilí or similar for a live Trad session (sessions warm up ~18:30–19:00 — arriving at 18:30 is perfect timing)
Notes
  • 🔴 MUST PRE-BOOK: Newgrange (Brú na Bóinne) — book via heritageireland.ie. Booking opens 30 days before your visit (from 29 March 2026). Slots fill fast. Book the 13:30 or 14:00 time slot to match this day's timeline. You cannot rush or miss the timed slot.
  • 📋 Castle Ward / Winterfell archery: pre-book via Clearsky Adventure Centre — experience is capacity-limited.
  • ✂️ If significantly running late: Castle Ward is the one to cut. Newgrange has a fixed timed slot you cannot miss — it is the irreplaceable experience on this day.
