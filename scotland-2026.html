Skye — The Full Trotternish Loop
Hebridean - Rooms & Apartments
Full day
▾
Morning
  • 08:30 Early start from Sleat to beat the crowds
  • 09:30 Old Man of Storr — 30 min hike on a clear path to the base of the pinnacles. Manageable for a 6yo, no summit needed.
  • 11:30 Kilt Rock & Mealt Falls — quick roadside viewpoint (10 min)
Afternoon
  • 12:00 LUNCH: The Hungry Gull (⭐ 4.8) or Columba 1400 (⭐ 4.5) in Staffin. Perfect timing halfway up the loop, spacious and spectacular sea views.
  • 13:15 The Quiraing — walk the flat section of the path for mind-blowing views over the landslip landscape
  • 14:45 Fairy Glen near Uig — a bizarre miniature landscape of grassy spirals and rocky towers. Basically a natural playground for a 6yo.
  • 16:00 CAFÉ & CATCH-UP: Uig Hotel Lounge (⭐ 4.5) — warm, spacious sanctuary to grab tea and thaw out after the windy fairy glen.
Evening
  • 17:00 Drive back south through Portree to Sleat
  • 18:00 DINNER: Skeabost House Hotel (⭐ 4.6) — spacious dining room on the route back down, perfect to break up the drive.
Notes
  • ✂️ If weather is poor or energy is low: Kilt Rock is a quick 10-min roadside stop — the most skippable. Storr, Quiraing, and Fairy Glen are the three essentials of this day.
  • Fairy Glen: free, no booking. Small parking area near Uig — arrive before 16:00 to avoid the busiest period.
  • ℹ️ Dunvegan Castle has moved to Day 7 morning — it fits the West Skye day perfectly and removes the long backtrack from today.
  • ⚠️ April on the Trotternish ridge is fully exposed — waterproofs and warm layers are non-negotiable, especially for the 6yo.
